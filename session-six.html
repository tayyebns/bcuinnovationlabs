Session 6 · 24 April 2026

Intellectual Property and Legal Awareness

Week 6 focused on the legal foundations behind building, helping founders understand how to protect and manage their ideas in real-world scenarios.

Date
24 April 2026
Type
Legal
Led by
BCU Pro Bono (League of Justice)
Contributor
Gia Dhami
Overview

Moving beyond building into protection.

This session shifted focus from creating ideas to protecting them. Founders were introduced to the legal foundations behind their work, with a focus on intellectual property, ownership, and risk.

The pace was practical and applied, pushing teams to think quickly about what they own, what could be at risk, and the implications of sharing or scaling ideas without proper protection.

Purpose

The workshop challenged teams to think beyond just building and into ownership. It encouraged participants to question not just what they create, but what they legally own, how it can be protected, and where risks could emerge if it is not secured properly.

Approach

Through practical scenarios and applied discussions, teams made quick decisions around intellectual property, licensing, and legal exposure. The focus was on clarity, risk awareness, and understanding how legal frameworks shape what founders can protect and scale.

Key takeaways

What participants developed.

Teams shifted from just building ideas to understanding how to protect and manage them in real-world contexts.

01

Ownership

Participants explored what they actually own, from code to branding, and how intellectual property rights apply to their work.

02

Protection

Teams learned how to safeguard their ideas through copyrights, trademarks, and basic legal structures to reduce risk.

03

Risk Awareness

The session highlighted common legal pitfalls, helping participants understand how poor legal decisions can impact growth, funding, and long-term control.
