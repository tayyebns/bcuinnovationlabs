Session 2 · 13 March 2026

Enterprise Fundamentals and Startup Advice

Week 2 introduced participants to the core principles of building a startup, led by the BCU Student Enterprise team.

Date
13 March 2026
Type
Validation
Led by
BCU Student Enterprise
Focus
Problem-solution fit
Overview

Turning early ideas into structured opportunities.

The session focused on turning early ideas into structured opportunities. Teams explored how to clearly define their value proposition, understand their target audience, and begin validating whether their solution solves a real problem. Emphasis was placed on moving beyond assumptions and grounding ideas in real user needs.

Purpose

This session helped participants move from energy and instinct into more disciplined startup thinking. It introduced the idea that strong ventures are built not just on creativity, but on evidence, clarity, and user relevance.

Approach

Teams were encouraged to test their thinking, question their assumptions, and define their value more clearly. The workshop provided practical frameworks that could be applied immediately to refine ideas carried forward from Week 1.

Key concepts

What the session introduced.

Participants were introduced to practical startup concepts that support stronger early-stage decision making.

01

Value proposition

Teams worked on defining what makes their solution useful, relevant, and worth paying attention to.

02

Customer discovery

The workshop introduced ways of understanding real user needs instead of relying on guesswork.

03

Validation

Participants began assessing whether their idea solves a real problem and has the foundations of something viable.

Outcome

Clearer direction, stronger structure.

By the end of the workshop, participants had a clearer direction, stronger problem-solution fit, and a more structured approach to developing their ventures.

Why it mattered

Throughout the session, teams were encouraged to think critically about viability, scalability, and impact, laying the groundwork for building solutions that are not just innovative but also realistic.
