Session 4 · 27 March 2026

Business Ideation Using Design Thinking

Week 4 shifted focus towards how ideas are built and scaled through systems thinking.

Date
27 March 2026
Type
Systems thinking
Focus
Scalability and structure
Outcome
Buildable foundations
Overview

Thinking beyond surface-level features.

The session introduced participants to the fundamentals of system design, encouraging teams to think beyond surface-level features and consider how all parts of their solution connect. This included understanding user flows, data movement, and how different components interact within a product.

Purpose

This workshop helped teams shift from simple ideas into more structured product thinking. It encouraged participants to see their ventures not as isolated features, but as systems made up of connected parts with clear roles.

Approach

Teams explored how to design solutions that are not only functional, but also scalable and efficient. Emphasis was placed on breaking down complex problems into manageable parts, identifying dependencies, and ensuring that each element of the system serves a clear purpose.

Key takeaways

What participants developed.

The workshop encouraged longer-term thinking around growth, functionality, and technical structure.

01

User flows

Teams considered how users would move through their product and how each step should connect logically.

02

Dependencies

Participants explored how features, data, and processes depend on one another inside a working system.

03

Scalability

Teams began thinking ahead about how their product would handle growth, increased usage, and future development.

Outcome

Clearer systems, stronger foundations.

By the end of the session, teams had a clearer technical and structural understanding of their solutions, along with a stronger foundation for development.

Why it mattered

The workshop helped shift mindsets from simple ideas to structured, buildable systems. It gave participants a more mature way of thinking about how products work in practice and how they can grow over time.
