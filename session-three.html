Session 3 · 20 March 2026

Brand Identity and Positioning

Week 3 focused on how startups present themselves, led by the BCU Business School.

Date
20 March 2026
Type
Branding
Led by
BCU Business School
Focus
Perception and communication
Overview

Moving beyond the idea itself.

The session explored the importance of building a strong and consistent brand identity. Participants learned how to clearly communicate who they are, what they stand for, and why their solution matters. Teams were encouraged to move beyond just having an idea, and instead think about how that idea is perceived by their audience.

Purpose

This workshop helped teams understand that how a venture is presented is as important as what it does. It pushed participants to think about identity, trust, and how ideas are interpreted by real users.

Approach

A key focus was on positioning. Teams worked on defining their place in the market, understanding their differentiators, and articulating their value in a way that resonates with users. This included refining messaging, tone, and overall brand direction.

Key takeaways

What participants strengthened.

Teams applied branding concepts directly to their own ideas, shaping how they present their ventures moving forward.

01

Identity

Participants worked on how to define who they are and what they stand for in a way that feels clear and consistent.

02

Positioning

Teams explored where they sit in the market and how to communicate what makes them different.

03

Trust

The session showed how strong branding can build trust, improve user engagement, and support long-term growth.

Outcome

Stronger positioning, clearer voice.

By the end of the session, participants had a clearer brand identity, stronger positioning, and more confidence in how to communicate their ideas.

Why it mattered

The session highlighted that good branding is not decoration. It shapes perception, helps ideas become legible to others, and supports the wider growth of a startup over time.
